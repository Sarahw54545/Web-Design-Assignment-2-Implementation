Advanced Entry

South East Technological University offers advanced entry into a range of undergraduate courses to those who already hold third-level educational qualifications or have significant professional experience, and to those who are currently completing, or have successfully completed, a minimum of one year of study at Level 6, 7 or 8 in a Higher Education Institution or an equivalent.

Mature Students

A mature applicant is an applicant who is aged 23 and over on the 1st January of the year in which they seek admission.

Applicants apply via the CAO website. To be considered under the mature route, there are two closing dates: 1 February and 1 May. Selection process involves being allocated places by SETU based on its mature applicant selection process. Candidates are assessed on evidence which has been submitted to the CAO.

Admission to courses may also be subject to interview and/or portfolio. Mature Applicants who are unsuccessful in attaining a place through the mature route may still compete on Leaving Certificate or QQI FET/FETAC points in CAO Round 1

Non EU Students

For Non-EU Applicants seeking admission to an SETU undergraduate program, adhere to the following steps:

  • Verify the entry requirements to ensure compliance with the general undergraduate criteria.
  • Applications are open from October each year for entry in September of the subsequent year.
  • SETU employs a rolling admission process, wherein the admission team continually reviews applications and releases admission decisions. Applying early is advisable to secure a place in the program.
  • Visa-required students must submit their applications before the 1st of June. Non-visa required applicants are encouraged to apply before the end of July. Your prompt application will contribute to a seamless process and enhance your chances of securing enrolment in SETU's programs.
  • If you are applying through an international agent or intend to do so, please consult our list of International Agents located in your vicinity.

What is Common Entry?

The Common Entry Scheme within Exercise Sciences offers a unique pathway for students to embark on their academic journey with a broad yet focused approach. Over the initial two years of study, participants delve into a comprehensive curriculum designed to provide a robust foundation in various aspects of Exercise Science.

Through a combination of theoretical exploration and hands-on practical placements, students gain invaluable insights into the multifaceted realm of exercise science. This immersive experience serves as a springboard for students to explore diverse career avenues within the field, ranging from fitness and movement to nutrition and positive psychology.

By exposing students to a wide array of subjects, the Common Entry Scheme equips them with the knowledge and skills necessary to make informed decisions regarding their specialization. As students progress through the program, they have the opportunity to tailor their academic trajectory by selecting one of three specialized exit pathways:

  • BSc (Hons) in Sport & Exercise Science
  • BSc (Hons) in Nutrition & Exercise Science
  • BSc (Hons) in Health & Exercise Science

This flexible structure empowers students to customize their educational experience based on their interests, passions, and career aspirations, ensuring they are well-prepared to excel in their chosen field upon graduation.
